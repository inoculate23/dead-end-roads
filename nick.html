Personal Correspondence · March 2026
Dear Nick,

This letter is a thank you. That is all it is meant to be, and we want to say so clearly at the start — because the rest of what we have on our minds can wait, and this part cannot.

We wrote a song about Point Roberts. We called it Dead End Roads — a folk song built on the melody of Country Roads, honest about the hard parts of life on this peninsula and equally honest about the people who make it worth staying for. You are in it.

"Nick Kiniski holds the bar like he held the wrestling ring"

That line is about something we actually noticed. There is a particular kind of authority — not loud, not performed — that comes from having held a room under pressure, from knowing what it takes to stand in the middle of something difficult and keep it together through sheer presence. You carried that quality out of the ring and into The Reef, and it shows. The bar is still standing. So is the community around it. That is not nothing, especially in a winter like this one has been.

Craig wants to say something directly: your support over the past while has meant more than it probably appeared to on the surface. When someone is going through a hard stretch and the people around them choose to show up anyway — quietly, without making it a transaction — that registers. It stays. You have been that kind of person, and Craig does not take it lightly.

We are also aware that the economics of running a place like The Reef in Point Roberts right now are not easy. The boycott has changed the shape of everything here, and a bar that depends on foot traffic from across the border has felt that more than most. We want you to know that we see that, and that we are thinking about what it looks like to be genuinely useful — not in a vague someday way, but in a real one. We have some ideas around live music and entertainment that we would like to talk through with you when the time feels right. Nothing is being proposed in this letter — that conversation belongs in person, over a drink, on your terms. This is simply to say the thought is there and the intention is good.

But that is not the point of today. The point of today is the song, and the fact that you are in it, and the fact that we are glad you are here keeping the lights on at The Reef. Point Roberts needs what that place is. And we look forward to supporting it any way we can as the season turns.

We have enclosed a working edit below — you will hear your line in the second verse. The recording is still being refined before mastering, but the words will not change.
