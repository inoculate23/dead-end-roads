Personal Correspondence · March 2026
Dear Pam,

There are two reasons this letter exists. The first is a song. The second is larger than that, and it has been waiting to be said properly for a while now.

We wrote a folk song about Point Roberts — about what it actually feels like to live here, the economics and the isolation and the long winter and the people who make it worth staying anyway. It is called Dead End Roads, and you are in it.

"Pam brings every letter like it's something worth delivering"

That line is about the way you show up — the care you carry into what could be just a job, the fact that the mail in Point Roberts arrives with a human being attached to it rather than just dropped on a step. In a place this isolated, the person who moves between houses and keeps the connections between people and the rest of the world is doing something that deserves to be named. We named it.

But here is the part that matters more.

When Craig left for Southeast Asia last autumn, he had to leave behind the two creatures he loves most in this world. That is not a small thing for anyone who has ever known what it is to love an animal — the particular trust you place in the person watching over them, the way you have to decide, quietly, who in your life is actually that person. Craig did not have to think long. There was only one name. Yours.

Point Roberts is a small town, and small towns have a way of sorting people into categories over time — allies and adversaries, the ones who stay close and the ones who keep distance. Craig will be honest with you: there are days here when the adversary column feels longer than it should. That kind of loneliness is its own specific weight, different from being alone in a city, harder in some ways because everyone knows everyone and the distances between people are measured in something other than miles.

Which is why what you gave him — your friendship, your reliability, your willingness to look after his babies without being asked twice — is not something that can be repaid with a line in a song. Though you have one of those too, and it is a good one. What it can be met with is honesty: you are one of the genuinely good people in this place, and Craig is grateful you are here, and so is everyone who has ever had a letter delivered by someone who treated it like it mattered.

We have included the song below. You will hear yourself in it. The recording is still being shaped before its final release, but the words will not change. Neither will what they mean.
