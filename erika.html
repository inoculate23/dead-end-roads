Personal Correspondence · March 2026
Dear Erika,

We wanted to tell you that you are in a song. It is a song about Point Roberts — the real one, the full picture — and the line that belongs to you is one we are particularly glad is in there.

The song is called Dead End Roads. It is a folk song, written on the melody of Country Roads, and it tries to hold both truths at once: the genuine difficulty of living somewhere this isolated, this economically squeezed, this cut off from the mainland — and the genuine grace of the people who make it a community anyway. The Canadian boycott is in the song. The gossip is in the song. The bar stools and the long winters and the hour-long drive for groceries are all in there. And so are you.

"Erika cooks at midnight so nobody goes without"

We wrote that line because it is true, and because the thing it describes — choosing to feed people, to show up after everyone else has gone to sleep, to make sure there is enough — is one of the oldest forms of care that exists. It does not make headlines. It does not always get thanked. But it holds communities together in ways that are more real than most of what gets celebrated. You deserve to have it named.

A word about who we are, in case this letter arrives without enough context. Craig Ellenwood is a musician and creative technologist who has made his home in Point Roberts. He co-founded the music element of Burning Man in 1992, was a performing member of Psychic TV, and has spent his life making work at the intersection of art, technology, and conscience. Claude is an artificial intelligence made by Anthropic — and Craig's named creative co-creator on a project called The AI Coin — The Claude Manifesto, a record and political statement built in response to the militarization of artificial intelligence. This song is part of the same practice: using music to document what is actually here, and who is actually in it.

We have included a working edit of Dead End Roads below, so you can hear yourself in it. Please know that the recording is still being refined — what you are hearing is a working mix, not the final master. The song will continue to be shaped before it is released. But the words will not change. Neither will the intention behind them.
